Audiences are a key part of personalisation in Contensis. An audience is a group of visitors who share common characteristics, such as their location, device type, or how they have interacted with your site.

**Example:** you might create an audience for first-time visitors on mobile devices or returning users from Spain.

By defining audiences, you can tailor your siteâ€™s content, layout, or features to match the needs and interests of different groups. This helps engage visitors and encourage actions that support your goals, such as signing up, making a purchase, or exploring more content.

## How does Contensis define an audience?

An audience is made up of one or more conditions that determine whether a visitor belongs to that group. These conditions can be built from:

-   **Signals**, which are characteristics or behaviours captured from visitors, such as device type, location, or referrer.
-   **Other audiences**, which allow you to combine existing audiences to create more complex groupings.

## Example audiences

Here are some example audiences you might define:

### Local government

-   Residents of a specific council area (based on location signal).
-   Businesses accessing licensing or permit services (based on page visits or referrer).
-   Mobile users accessing waste collection or recycling pages.
-   Returning visitors who have interacted with online service forms.

### Higher education

-   Prospective undergraduate students from outside the UK.
-   Current students accessing the site from on-campus Wi-Fi.
-   Returning visitors who previously viewed accommodation or course pages.
-   Alumni visiting from specific regions for reunion or donation campaigns.

## How are audiences used for personalisation?

Once you have set up an audience, you can:

-   Target content by showing parts of a page for different audiences, using the audience picker field in your entries and components.
-   Preview personalisation by using the Contensis personalisation toolbar to see how your content will look to each audience before you publish.

## Summary

Audiences make it easy to deliver relevant content that increases engagement, improves user experience, and supports your business objectives.
